# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

The Tacua community is committed to a respectful, safe, and inclusive environment for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience, or ability.

Expected behavior includes constructive technical disagreement, respect for boundaries, clear attribution, and attention to the privacy and safety of users and contributors.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, stalking, sexualized attention, sustained disruption, publishing another person's private information, or retaliation against someone who raises a concern in good faith.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository activity, project communication, community events, and public interactions where someone is representing Tacua.

## Reporting and enforcement

Do not disclose a conduct report in a public issue or pull request. Use a private project contact published by the repository. Until one is published, use GitHub's private **Report content** or **Report abuse** flow so the concern is not exposed publicly.

Project maintainers are responsible for fair, timely, and confidential review. They may remove content, issue a warning, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently exclude a participant, proportionate to the behavior and its impact. Information will be shared only as needed to investigate and respond, subject to legal and safety obligations.

Good-faith reports and participation in an investigation must not result in retaliation. Deliberately false or bad-faith reports may themselves violate this policy.

Security vulnerabilities and credible privacy risks should instead be reported through the private process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
